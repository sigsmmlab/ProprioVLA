\section{Introduction}

Vision-language-action (VLA) policies aim to map language instructions and
visual observations to robot actions, making them a promising interface for
generalist manipulation. Recent robot foundation models show that broad
pretraining and large robot datasets can yield policies that transfer across
tasks and embodiments~\citep{brohan2022rt1,brohan2023rt2,team2024octo,kim2024openvla}.
For manipulation, however, success depends on more than recognizing objects in
the image: the policy must relate visual evidence to the robot's own
proprioceptive state and to the geometry of the surrounding scene. Spatial
robustness under changes in visual perspective is therefore a core requirement
for reliable VLA control.

Despite this progress, we find that fine-tuned VLA policies can remain weakly
grounded in their own embodiment. In controlled camera-perturbation evaluations
where the underlying robot-scene state is unchanged, lateral camera shifts
induce co-directional shifts in predicted robot trajectories. This systematic
failure suggests that current VLAs may exploit image-space shortcuts, such as
pixel-coordinate correlations, rather than grounding actions in proprioceptive
state and robot-object geometry. Such shortcuts are especially problematic
during fine-tuning, where a policy may match demonstrations under a fixed camera
distribution while remaining brittle when the same physical state is viewed
from a different perspective.

To address this issue, we introduce \textsc{ProprioVLA}, a self-supervised
learning framework for proprioceptive grounding in VLA policies. The central
idea is to guide the policy to visually attend to the robot itself by
conditioning visual queries on proprioceptive state. Rather than treating the
state as the supervision signal, \textsc{ProprioVLA} uses state as a guiding
input and obtains supervision from state-aligned residual flow. This signal
captures residual visual motion that is both locally salient in the image and
synchronized with proprioceptive change, revealing where the robot's own visual
evidence appears during interaction.

\textsc{ProprioVLA} turns this signal into confidence-weighted self-grounding
heatmaps and uses them to train two complementary objectives. The heatmaps are
mined from robot interaction by selecting residual motion that is salient in the
image and aligned with proprioceptive change, abstaining from supervision when
the signal is uncertain. They supervise a visual self-grounding objective that
aligns state-conditioned attention with robot-related visual evidence. A
complementary proprioceptive reconstruction objective requires the attended
representation to recover the robot's own state, encouraging the grounded
visual representation to remain tied to proprioception. Together, these
objectives incentivize proprioceptive grounding without manual annotations,
external grounding datasets, or supervision from stronger foundation models.

We evaluate \textsc{ProprioVLA} in both real-robot and simulation environments,
including controlled camera-perturbation settings that directly test spatial
robustness. Across these evaluations, \textsc{ProprioVLA} improves task success
and robustness over action-supervised VLA adaptation. Ablations further support
the role of both components: self-grounding heatmaps provide spatial supervision
for state-conditioned attention, while proprioceptive reconstruction anchors the
attended representation to the robot state. These results suggest that
self-supervised proprioceptive grounding is a practical route toward spatially
reliable VLA control.

Our contributions are threefold:
\begin{itemize}
    \item We identify a systematic grounding failure in VLA policies: camera
    shifts can induce co-directional shifts in predicted robot trajectories,
    revealing reliance on image-space shortcuts rather than proprioceptive
    grounding.
    \item We introduce state-aligned residual flow as an implicit supervision
    signal for self-grounding, enabling confidence-weighted heatmaps to be mined
    from robot interaction without manual annotations, external grounding
    datasets, or stronger foundation-model supervision.
    \item We propose \textsc{ProprioVLA}, a self-supervised training framework
    that couples state-conditioned visual attention with proprioceptive
    reconstruction to improve task success and robustness in real-robot and
    simulation environments.
\end{itemize}
